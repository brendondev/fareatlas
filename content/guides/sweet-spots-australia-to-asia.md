A "sweet spot" is a route where an award chart is priced below what the cash fare — and the flying distance — would justify. Australia to Asia is thick with them, because the flights are medium-haul but the cabins are long-haul quality. This guide names the ones worth building a trip around.

## Why Asia is the best-value premium region from Australia

A Sydney–Tokyo Business seat is a genuine flat-bed, overnight, full-service product — but the distance is only around 4,800 miles. Many award charts price by distance band, so you're paying a mid-tier points cost for a top-tier seat. That mismatch is the whole opportunity.

## The standout routes

### East coast to Tokyo and Seoul

The overnight flights from Sydney, Melbourne and Brisbane to Tokyo (both Haneda and Narita) and Seoul are the classic sweet spot. Business awards here regularly clear **4+ cents per point** against cash fares, and premium inventory is more open than on the ultra-competitive routes to Europe.

Booking window: premium award seats on these routes open around **11–12 months out** and again in a second release roughly **6–8 weeks** before departure when airlines adjust inventory. Miss the first, watch for the second.

### Perth as a hidden gateway

Perth's proximity to Southeast Asia makes Perth–Singapore and Perth–Kuala Lumpur unusually cheap on points for the seat quality. These are short enough to sit in the lowest distance band on several charts while still being served by wide-body aircraft with proper Business cabins.

### The Singapore connection play

Routing through Singapore to reach a second Asian city on a single award can cost the same points as the Singapore leg alone on some charts, effectively making the onward flight free in points terms. This only works on programs that price by origin-and-destination rather than per-segment — knowing which is which is the advanced skill.

## Which points to use

- **Domestic-earned points** transfer most efficiently to the programs of the airlines actually flying these routes. Earning flexibly (through a transferable currency) beats being locked to one airline.
- Watch the **taxes** on fuel-surcharge-heavy carriers — a sweet-spot points price can be undone by $400 in surcharges. The genuinely great redemptions pair a low points cost with low taxes.

## Timing the booking

1. **11–12 months out:** the first inventory release. Best for locking a specific date, especially over school holidays.
2. **6–8 weeks out:** the second release and schedule-change window. Best for flexible travellers hunting a bargain.
3. **Last 2 weeks:** airlines dump unsold premium seats into award inventory. High risk, but the single best time for a spontaneous Business seat.

## Building the trip

Anchor the trip on the sweet-spot leg — the premium overnight flight — and fill the rest with cheaper Economy awards or cash where points are weak. The cents-per-point discipline from the starter guides applies to every leg: spend points where they clear 2 cents, pay cash where they don't. A great award trip is rarely all-points; it's points on the legs that deserve them.
